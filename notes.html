Calvin Cycle

Light Reactions:

Inputs: H20, Sunlight

Outputs: 02, ATP & NADPH

Dark Reactions

Inputs: C02, ATP, NADPH

Outputs: C6H12O6 (Sugar)

The Three Stages of The Calvin Cycle

  1. Carbon Fixation
  2. Reduction Phase
  3. Regeneration of RuBP

Process

RuBP - 5 Carbon, Two Phosphate

6C02(1 C each) + 6RuBP(5 C each) = 6RuBisCO(6 C each)

Add carbon dioxide, the essential molecule for photosynthesis.

6 C (unstable) splits into two 3 C compounds

This compound is called PGA

ATP and NADPH then get added to PGA to create G3P (Phosphate gets removed, 2 Hydrogens are added)

One G3P goes to make glucose, and the other 5 go back to make new RuBP

Stomata

Stomata are pores in the plant that let in C02

In places where it is very hot, plants cannot open their stomata in the day

Instead, they let in C02 only at night and store it for later.

C02 + PEP --> Oxaloacetic acid.

At night, the above reactionis reversed to restore the C02.
